# Gravity, Inflation, and Hidden Sectors

Gravity is not only the background geometry of the early Universe. It also organizes the effective field theories used for inflation and mediates unavoidable interactions between otherwise hidden sectors and the visible or cosmological plasma.

This page has two related branches. The first concerns inflationary effective field theories with scalar fields coupled to gravity. The second concerns gravitational production of particles, dark sectors, dark radiation, or gravitons after inflation.

## Question

How should scalar fields coupled to gravity be described consistently, and what cosmological consequences follow from gravitational couplings that cannot be switched off?

For inflationary EFTs, the issue is frame-independent consistency: nonminimal coupling, field-space geometry, cutoff scales, and perturbative unitarity. For hidden sectors, the issue is production through the expanding or oscillating gravitational background, even when direct non-gravitational interactions are absent or very small.

## Why it matters

Inflationary models often use scalar fields in regimes where gravitational interactions are part of the effective theory. Understanding the field-space geometry and unitarity structure is necessary for judging the consistency of the model and the reliability of predictions.

At the same time, gravity can produce particles after inflation through oscillating backgrounds or curvature couplings. Such production can populate hidden sectors, contribute to dark matter or dark radiation, or generate gravitons and high-frequency stochastic gravitational waves.

## Key ideas

**Scalar-gravity EFT and inflation.** Nonminimal coupling, Higgs inflation, Einstein-Cartan gravity, field-space geometry, and perturbative unitarity are different aspects of the same consistency question. The goal is to state physical conclusions in frame-independent language and to understand which cutoff scales are background-dependent.

**Gravitational production and hidden sectors.** Oscillating scale factors, inflaton backgrounds, and curvature operators can create particles gravitationally. This includes hidden sectors coupled only through gravity, possible dark matter or dark radiation production, graviton production, and curvature-squared operators induced by heavy particles.

This direction touches reheating because particle production happens after inflation, but it is not organized around thermalization. Its focus is on what gravity itself implies for EFT consistency and for unavoidable production channels.

## Representative papers

<div class="record-list" markdown="1">

### Scalar-gravity EFT and inflation

- **[Violent Preheating in Inflation with Nonminimal Coupling](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.05209)**  
  Yohei Ema, Ryusuke Jinno, Kyohei Mukaida, and Kazunori Nakayama  
  JCAP 02, 045 (2017), arXiv:1609.05209  
  Shows how nonminimal coupling can affect post-inflationary particle production, linking scalar-gravity EFT structure to preheating.

- **[Quantum Corrections to Higgs Inflation in Einstein-Cartan Gravity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.14398)**  
  Minxi He, Kohei Kamada, and Kyohei Mukaida  
  JHEP 01, 014 (2024), arXiv:2308.14398  
  Studies quantum corrections to Higgs inflation in an Einstein-Cartan setting, where the gravitational variables change the scalar-field geometry.

- **[Geometry and Unitarity of Scalar Fields Coupled to Gravity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.15420)**  
  Minxi He, Kohei Kamada, and Kyohei Mukaida  
  Phys. Rev. Lett. 132, 191501 (2024), arXiv:2308.15420  
  Uses field-space geometry to formulate perturbative unitarity for scalar fields coupled to gravity in a frame-independent way.

- **[Revisiting unitarity of single scalar field with non-minimal coupling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06296)**  
  Minxi He, Muzi Hong, Kyohei Mukaida, and Tomoya Nishiki  
  arXiv:2603.06296  
  Refines the single-field nonminimal-coupling case using scattering amplitudes to identify which interactions control the relevant unitarity scale.

### Gravitational production and hidden sectors

- **[Gravitational Effects on Inflaton Decay](https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.02475)**  
  Yohei Ema, Ryusuke Jinno, Kyohei Mukaida, and Kazunori Nakayama  
  arXiv:1502.02475  
  Studies how gravitational interactions affect inflaton decay and particle production after inflation.

- **[Gravitational Particle Production in Oscillating Background and Its Cosmological Implications](https://arxiv.org/abs/1604.08898)**  
  Yohei Ema, Ryusuke Jinno, Kyohei Mukaida, and Kazunori Nakayama  
  Phys. Rev. D 94, 063517 (2016), arXiv:1604.08898  
  Analyzes particle production from oscillating backgrounds and its implications for sectors coupled gravitationally.

- **[Scalar field couplings to quadratic curvature and decay into gravitons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.12774)**  
  Yohei Ema, Kyohei Mukaida, and Kazunori Nakayama  
  arXiv:2112.12774  
  Studies curvature-squared couplings and decay into gravitons, connecting heavy physics to gravitational radiation.

## Current direction

The current direction is to clarify both sides of the gravity interface: the consistency of scalar-gravity EFTs used for inflation, and the unavoidable cosmological consequences of gravitational couplings to hidden particles, dark radiation, or gravitons.
