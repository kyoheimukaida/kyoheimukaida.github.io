# Gravity, Inflation, and Field-Space Geometry

This direction is about the theoretical consistency of inflationary effective field theories involving scalar fields coupled to gravity. In the broader research program, it asks when the scalar-gravity descriptions used during and after inflation are predictive, frame-independent, and under perturbative control.

## Question

How should scalar fields coupled to gravity be described consistently as effective field theories during and after inflation?

The question is especially sharp for nonminimal couplings and Higgs-inflation-like models, where frame choices, field redefinitions, and background-dependent cutoff scales can obscure what is physically invariant.

## Why it matters

Inflationary models often use scalar fields in regimes where gravitational interactions are part of the effective theory, not a small afterthought. Understanding the field-space geometry and unitarity structure is necessary for judging the consistency of the model, the meaning of quantum corrections, and the reliability of post-inflationary predictions.

## Key ideas

The technical branches include gravitational particle production in oscillating backgrounds, nonminimal coupling, Higgs inflation, Einstein-Cartan gravity, frame-independent field-space geometry, and perturbative unitarity. The common issue is how to phrase scalar-gravity dynamics in terms of physical, not merely frame-dependent, quantities.

This direction overlaps with reheating through particle production and preheating in nonminimally coupled models. Its organizing question, however, is EFT consistency and geometry rather than thermal history.

  Minxi He, Kohei Kamada, and Kyohei Mukaida

## Current direction

The current direction is to clarify which statements about inflationary scalar-gravity theories are invariant, which are background-dependent, and which are artifacts of variables. This helps determine when such effective theories remain predictive during inflation and in the transition to post-inflationary dynamics.
