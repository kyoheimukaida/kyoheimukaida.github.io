# Primordial Black Holes and Gravitational-Wave Probes

This direction is about compact objects and gravitational signals as probes of small-scale early-Universe physics. In the broader research program, primordial black holes connect inflationary fluctuations, possible relic abundances, gravitational-wave signatures, and local plasma effects around evaporating black holes.

## Question

What can primordial black holes and their gravitational or plasma signatures tell us about small-scale primordial fluctuations and early-Universe dynamics?

Primordial black holes are not assumed to be established dark matter. They are useful because their formation, abundance, gravitational-wave signals, and evaporation products are all sensitive to physics at scales that are difficult to access by other cosmological probes.

## Why it matters

PBHs can test enhanced small-scale curvature perturbations, motivate gravitational-wave signatures, and constrain inflationary scenarios. Even when they are subdominant or absent, the observational limits on them help define what small-scale primordial structure is allowed.

## Key ideas

The technical branches are centered on PBHs. One branch studies enhanced small-scale curvature perturbations, PBH formation, and possible dark matter roles. Another connects the same perturbations to induced gravitational waves relevant to LIGO- and PTA-motivated discussions. A more recent branch studies the environment around small evaporating PBHs, where Hawking emission can create local hot spots and nonequilibrium plasma dynamics.

This page remains PBH-centered. PBH formation touches inflationary perturbations and PBH evaporation touches thermalization, but those connections are used here to understand compact-object probes rather than to merge this direction with primordial correlator theory.

## Current direction

The current direction is to use PBHs as probes without relying on a single interpretation of them. This means tracking both formation-era signatures, such as induced gravitational waves, and evaporation-era signatures, such as local thermalization around small PBHs.
