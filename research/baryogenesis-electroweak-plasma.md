# Baryogenesis, Leptogenesis, Lepton Flavor, and Electroweak Plasma

This direction is about how matter-antimatter asymmetry is generated and processed in the hot electroweak plasma. In the broader research program, it follows charges, flavors, and approximate conservation laws through the thermal history rather than treating baryogenesis as a single isolated reaction.

## Question

How can microscopic charge-violating and flavor-sensitive processes in the early plasma lead to the baryon asymmetry of the Universe?

The question involves both generation and redistribution. An asymmetry may be produced in one charge sector, partially erased by washout, transferred between flavors, or converted into baryon number by electroweak sphalerons.

## Why it matters

The baryon asymmetry is a small number, but it is a basic boundary condition for the later Universe. It is also sensitive to high-energy interactions that may be hard to test directly. Thermal field theory matters because chemical potentials, spectator effects, flavor equilibration, and sphalerons determine which microscopic asymmetry is actually visible as baryon number.

## Key ideas

The technical tools are those of a reacting plasma: chemical potentials, spectator effects, wash-in and washout processes, lepton flavor equilibration, and anomalous electroweak conversion. These tools make it possible to follow approximate conserved charges even when no single particle species carries the whole story.

The papers in this category use several mechanisms, including axion-driven dynamics, wash-in leptogenesis, and lepton-flavor violation. The common point is not any one mechanism, but the idea that baryogenesis is controlled by how charges move through the electroweak plasma.

## Representative papers

<div class="record-list" markdown="1">

- **[Baryogenesis from axion inflation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.13318)**  
  Valerie Domcke, Benedict von Harling, Enrico Morgante, and Kyohei Mukaida  
  JCAP 10, 032 (2019), arXiv:1905.13318  
  Uses axion-inflation dynamics as a source of charge asymmetry processed by anomalous Standard Model reactions.

- **[Wash-In Leptogenesis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.09347)**  
  Valerie Domcke, Kohei Kamada, Kyohei Mukaida, Kai Schmitz, and Masaki Yamada  
  Phys. Rev. Lett. 126, 201802 (2021), arXiv:2011.09347  
  Introduces wash-in as a way to generate baryon-minus-lepton asymmetry from preexisting chemical potentials rather than conventional decays.

- **[Leptoflavorgenesis: baryon asymmetry of the Universe from lepton flavor violation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.03082)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida, Kai Schmitz, and Masaki Yamada  
  Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 011803 (2022), arXiv:2111.03082  
  Shows that lepton flavor violation can be cosmologically relevant even when the total baryon-minus-lepton charge initially vanishes.

- **[A new constraint on primordial lepton flavour asymmetries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.03237)**  
  Valerie Domcke, Kohei Kamada, Kyohei Mukaida, Kai Schmitz, and Masaki Yamada  
  Phys. Rev. Lett. 130, 261803 (2023), arXiv:2208.03237  
  Turns the plasma evolution of lepton flavor asymmetries into a cosmological constraint.

- **[Thermal Wash-in Leptogenesis via Heavy Higgs Decay](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14332)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida, Hidenaga Watanabe, and Masaki Yamada  
  JCAP 09, 063 (2024), arXiv:2405.14332  
  Provides a concrete thermal realization of wash-in leptogenesis through heavy Higgs decay.

## Current direction

The current direction is a more systematic description of baryon and lepton number in the electroweak plasma. This includes established ingredients such as sphalerons, flavor equilibration, and washout, and possible extensions involving anomalous transport or chiral plasma effects as current or future work rather than settled conclusions.
