# Reheating, Preheating, and Thermalization

This direction is about the origin of the hot Big Bang state. In the broader research program, it asks how a cold, coherent post-inflationary background gives its energy to particles and how those particles become the radiation plasma assumed in later cosmology.

## Question

How does a cold, coherent, post-inflationary state become the hot radiation-dominated Universe?

The question has two layers. The first is reheating: how energy is transferred from an inflaton or another coherent background into particle excitations. The second is thermalization: how those excitations scatter, multiply, and approach a plasma described by temperature and transport coefficients.

## Why it matters

Many early-Universe calculations start after the plasma already exists. Reheating determines when that assumption becomes valid and what happens before it is valid. The answer feeds into dark matter production, baryogenesis, phase transitions, and model-dependent reheating histories.

## Key ideas

The program combines several mechanisms that operate at different stages. Dissipative effects and perturbative decays describe gradual energy loss from the coherent background. Nonperturbative preheating describes particle production driven by time-dependent masses or kinetic terms. Kinetic theory then describes how the produced particles form a plasma through scatterings, screening, splittings, and, in some regimes, LPM-suppressed radiation.

The point is not to replace the thermal history by a single reheating temperature. The useful description often asks which sector has received energy, which momenta have thermalized, and whether the reactions relevant for a given observable occur before or after full thermal equilibrium.

## Representative papers

<div class="record-list" markdown="1">

- **[Thermalization after/during Reheating](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.3097)**  
  Keisuke Harigaya and Kyohei Mukaida  
  JHEP 05, 006 (2014), arXiv:1312.3097  
  Shows how thermalization can proceed during reheating rather than only after it, and clarifies when the instantaneous-thermalization approximation is reliable.

- **[Dissipative Effects on Reheating after Inflation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1212.4985)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida and Kazunori Nakayama  
  JCAP 03, 002 (2013), arXiv:1212.4985  
  Studies finite-temperature dissipation as a mechanism for inflaton energy loss and radiation production.

- **[Thermalization Process after Inflation and Effective Potential of Scalar Field](https://arxiv.org/abs/1506.07661)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida and Masaki Yamada  
  JCAP 02, 003 (2016), arXiv:1506.07661  
  Uses incomplete thermalization to show how scalar-field dynamics can differ from estimates based on an already-thermal plasma.

- **[Violent Preheating in Inflation with Nonminimal Coupling](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.05209)**  
  Yohei Ema, Ryusuke Jinno, Kyohei Mukaida, and Kazunori Nakayama  
  JCAP 02, 045 (2017), arXiv:1609.05209  
  Provides an example where nonminimal coupling makes nonperturbative particle production important after inflation.

- **[Cascades of high-energy SM particles in the primordial thermal plasma](https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.11708)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida and Masaki Yamada  
  JHEP 10, 116 (2022), arXiv:2208.11708  
  Develops the kinetic description of energetic Standard Model particles after production, including medium effects relevant for reaction rates.

- **[Perturbative reheating and thermalization of pure Yang-Mills plasma](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14054)**  
  Kyohei Mukaida and Masaki Yamada  
  JHEP 05, 174 (2024), arXiv:2402.14054  
  Uses pure Yang-Mills theory as a controlled non-Abelian setting for studying perturbative reheating and plasma formation.

## Current direction

The current direction is to make reheating a calculable part of cosmological history rather than a boundary condition. This includes controlled plasma calculations, model-dependent energy-transfer mechanisms, and applications such as [Reheating after Axion Inflation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01228), where gauge-field production is one realization of the broader reheating problem.
