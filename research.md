# Research

My research studies how the present Universe was shaped by particle physics in the very early Universe. The common theme is the interface between microscopic quantum field theory and cosmological history: how the hot plasma formed, how charges and relics were generated in it, how small-scale fluctuations can be probed, and how inflationary effective theories remain consistent.

The six directions below are complementary parts of that program rather than isolated topics. They range from nonequilibrium plasma formation after inflation to the theoretical structure of primordial correlators and scalar fields coupled to gravity.

<div class="card-grid">

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/reheating-thermalization.html">Reheating, preheating, and thermalization</a></h3>
  <p>The origin of the hot Big Bang state: energy transfer from a coherent post-inflationary background into particles, followed by the formation of a thermal plasma.</p>
</article>

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/baryogenesis-electroweak-plasma.html">Baryogenesis, leptogenesis, lepton flavor, and electroweak plasma</a></h3>
  <p>The generation and redistribution of charges in the electroweak plasma, including lepton flavor, approximate conservation laws, wash-in and washout, and sphalerons.</p>
</article>

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/dark-matter-production.html">Dark matter production in nonstandard thermal histories</a></h3>
  <p>Relic-abundance theory for dark matter, from precision finite-temperature calculations in a thermal plasma to production in nonstandard or not-yet-thermal histories.</p>
</article>

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/primordial-black-holes.html">Primordial black holes and gravitational-wave probes</a></h3>
  <p>Compact objects and gravitational signatures as probes of small-scale primordial fluctuations, possible dark matter, and plasma effects near evaporating black holes.</p>
</article>

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/cosmological-collider-correlators.html">Cosmological collider physics and primordial correlators</a></h3>
  <p>Primordial correlation functions as high-energy probes, with emphasis on the in-in formalism, analytic structure, nonlocal signals, soft limits, and loop corrections.</p>
</article>

<article class="paper-card">
  <h3><a href="research/gravity-inflation-geometry.html">Gravity, inflation, and field-space geometry</a></h3>
  <p>Scalar-gravity EFT consistency in inflation, together with gravitational production of hidden particles, dark radiation, or gravitons after inflation.</p>
</article>
